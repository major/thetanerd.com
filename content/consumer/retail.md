Retail ETFs offer targeted exposure to specific consumer spending channels ‚Äî brick-and-mortar, e-commerce, restaurants, and leisure. Unlike XLY (dominated by Amazon/Tesla), retail-focused funds capture the breadth of consumer spending behavior.

## XRT ‚Äî The breadth play

XRT's **equal-weight methodology** is its superpower:

- Every retailer counts equally ‚Äî Dollar Tree = Amazon
- Small-cap tilt ‚Äî smaller retailers have meaningful impact  
- Breadth signal ‚Äî when XRT outperforms XLY, the *average* retailer is doing well

| XRT vs XLY | XRT | XLY |
|------------|-----|-----|
| Weighting | Equal | Cap-weighted |
| Amazon | ~1-2% | ~22% |
| Tesla | 0% | ~17% |
| Holdings | ~80 | ~50 |

## PEJ ‚Äî Experiential spending

PEJ targets companies benefiting from experiences over goods:

| Subsector | Weight | Examples |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Hotels & Resorts | ~25-30% | Marriott, Hilton |
| Restaurants | ~20-25% | McDonald's, Chipotle |
| Casinos & Gaming | ~15-20% | MGM, Wynn |
| Entertainment | ~10-15% | Disney, Live Nation |

**Outperforms when:** Strong employment, low gas prices, vacation seasons  
**Underperforms when:** Recession fears, high inflation, health concerns
