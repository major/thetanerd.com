By monitoring ratios between crude oil, E&P stocks, services companies, and midstream infrastructure, traders can identify sector rotation, capex cycle turns, and oil price inflections before they show up in headlines.

This page consolidates the key relative charts and trading signals across the energy complex into a single dashboard.

## Essential relative charts

### USO/SPY â€” Crude Oil vs. Market

The foundational ratio for understanding oil's macro role:

| USO/SPY Behavior | Signal | Interpretation |
|------------------|--------|----------------|
| Sharp spike up | Geopolitical shock | Supply disruption, risk premium |
| Steady grind up | Inflation trade | Commodity cycle, inflation concerns |
| Steady grind down | Demand destruction | Strong dollar, oversupply, recession |
| Sharp drop | Risk-off panic | Flight from commodities |

**When to use**: Macro regime identification. Rising USO/SPY = commodity inflation trade working.

### XOP/SPY â€” E&P vs. Market

Shows whether high-beta energy is attracting capital:

| XOP/SPY Behavior | Signal | Interpretation |
|------------------|--------|----------------|
| Rising | Energy rotation | Risk-on for commodities, inflation concerns |
| Falling | Growth preferred | Tech/growth leading, energy out of favor |
| Diverging from oil | Warning | Market doesn't trust the oil move |

**When to use**: Sector rotation timing. Rising XOP/SPY = capital flowing into energy.

### XOP/XLE â€” E&P vs. Integrated Majors

The **key ratio** for choosing between high-beta E&P and defensive integrated majors:

| XOP/XLE Behavior | Condition | Action |
|------------------|-----------|--------|
| Rising | Oil acceleration, backwardation | Favor XOP â€” high-beta wins |
| Falling | Oil weakness, contango | Favor XLE â€” integrated majors buffer losses |
| Breaking out | Sustained oil strength | Overweight XOP aggressively |
| Breaking down | Capex cuts, margin pressure | Rotate to XLE defensively |

**When to use**: Within-sector allocation. This tells you which energy exposure to favor.

### OIH/SPY â€” Oil Services vs. Market

### AMLP/SPY â€” Midstream vs. Market

## Cross-asset signals

### USO/GLD â€” Oil vs. Gold

Compares the "growth commodity" to the "fear commodity":

| USO/GLD Behavior | Signal | Interpretation |
|------------------|--------|----------------|
| Rising | Growth optimism | Industrial demand strong |
| Falling | Risk-off | Flight to safety, monetary inflation fears |
| At extremes | Mean reversion | Ratio tends to revert over 1-2 years |

**Historical context**: When gold buys 50+ barrels of oil (vs. ~15-20 average), oil is likely oversold.

### Oil/Dollar (USO/UUP)

Traditional inverse relationship (weakening since US became net exporter):

| Relationship | Signal | Caveat |
|--------------|--------|--------|
| Both falling | Risk-off | Classic correlation |
| Both rising | Supply shock | Geopolitics overriding dollar |
| Inverse moving | Normal | Verify with fundamentals |

**When to use**: Macro confirmation. Strong dollar typically pressures oil.

## Actionable strategies

### Strategy 1: Cycle Rotation

**Setup**: XOP/XLE breaks out from consolidation

**Entry**: Buy XOP on breakout confirmation (close above resistance)

**Management**: 
- Add on pullbacks to 20-day MA
- Rotate to XLE when XOP/XLE rolls over

**Exit**: XOP/XLE breaks below 50-day MA

### Strategy 2: Capex Cycle Trade

**Setup**: OIH/SPY bottoming after prolonged decline + E&P budget increases announced

**Entry**: Buy OIH when 50-day MA turns up

**Management**:
- Hold while rig count rising
- Watch utilization for pricing power

**Exit**: OIH/XOP at cycle highs + capex guidance softening

### Strategy 3: Income Rotation

**Setup**: AMLP yield > 8% + AMLP/SPY bottoming

**Entry**: Buy AMLP on higher low in ratio

**Management**:
- Hold for yield + distribution growth
- Monitor coverage ratios (want > 1.5x)

**Exit**: Yield compresses < 7% + risk-on rotation accelerating

### Strategy 4: Divergence Play

**Setup**: AMLP rising while oil falling (positive divergence)

**Entry**: Buy AMLP on confirmation of divergence (3+ weeks)

**Thesis**: Fee-based model proving resilient; market will reprice

**Exit**: Oil stabilizes and XLE catches up (divergence closes)
