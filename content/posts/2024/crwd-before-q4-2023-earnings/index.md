[CrowdStrike](https://www.crowdstrike.com/en-us/) provides a suite of information security technologies for businesses of all sizes.
It's no secret that security breaches are on the rise around the world and they seem to rotate between different sectors over the years.

As CRWD heads into its Q4 2024 earnings call tonight, let's examine the data from charts and institutional trade to see if there's more risk to the upside or downside.

{{< disclaimer >}}

🚨 Remember that ***earnings are dangerous.***

Price action during the earnings release and subsequent call will often surprise veteran traders.
Even if CRWD reports great numbers, the price could fall like a rock.
We could also see the price go through the roof if the numbers look terrible. 🤷‍♂️

## Charts

I start my chart digging on a weekly chart back to a useful landmark.

2022 was a rough patch for CRWD as interest rate fears gripped the market.
However, it bottomed out early in 2023 and took on a more confirmed bullish look as soon as the price blasted through a high volume level around $150.
The moving averages all had bullish crosses going into the fall of 2023 and CRWD rocketed up to $300 and beyond.

Today, CRWD sits above its volume weighted average price (VWAP) lines from January 1st, 2024 and from the last earnings call.

Let's take a look at CRWD since the 2023 bottom which some additional indicators enabled:

The volume by price (VBP) indicator on the right _(horizontal blue lines)_ shows a huge amount of volume from $100-$160 with the point of control _(red line)_ around $130.
This suggests the highest volume is still around $130 and 70% of the volume traded since the bottom is between about $90-$170.
**I'd really like to see higher volume above $170.**

At the bottom of the chart, we have three indicators.
From top to bottom:

* TTM Squeeze: The squeeze is still on (opening trades or keeping existing trades open is indicated), but the strength is waning.
* On balance volume (OBV): Volume still supports the upwards move, but it's losing some strength as the moving average gets closer to the volume line.
* Coppock Curve: The rate of change is weakening, but it's still quite positive.

These don't strike me as _bearish_ right off the bat, but they are fairly consistent with a stock that is waiting on news or starting a new consolidation pattern.
This could be one of those moments where the market is holding its breath until we get the earnings numbers.

Recent earnings calls in the cybersecurity sector have hinted at wildly different results depending on the company.
There's been a ton of news recently about [Nancy Pelosi buying PANW calls](https://www.benzinga.com/general/politics/24/03/37460893/pelosi-loves-palo-alto-networks-stock-so-do-these-other-top-performing-traders-in-congress) along with other members of the US Congress.

## Risk to the upside

There are several areas in the weekly chart that lead me to think there's risk to the upside:

* CRWD sits above the YTD and earnings VWAP lines.
* All three moving averages are stacked properly. _(shortest time frames on top, longest time frames on the bottom)_
* All three moving averages have an upward slope.
* CRWD started its rally from a solid base around $140-$160 that was thoroughly tested before the move started.
* All three indicators on the bottom of the chart are still in bullish territory.

## Risk to the downside

The main issue I see to the downside is the lack of volume.
Much of the rise to $300 was done on volume that is dwarfed by the volume below $200.

## Institutional trades

[Volume Leaders](https://volumeleaders.com) is a good source for tracking large trades made on various stocks and ETFs.
Although it doesn't tell you the directionality of the trades made, it can give you a hint about when institutions are making trades and how large their bets are.

Here's a yearly chart for CRWD:

{{< figure
    src="crwd-vl-1y.png"
    href="crwd-vl-1y.png"
    alt="CRWD 1Y Volume Leaders chart"
    caption="CRWD 1Y Volume Leaders chart"
    >}}

Blue circles are lit exchange trades, or trades made live on the stock exchange.
Orange circles represent dark pool trades that are made off the exchange and reported later.
The numbers inside the circles represent the rank of the trade relative to all trades made on that stock since it began trading.
A #46 means it's the 46th biggest trade made since CRWD started trading.

Starting from the left, CRWD had some big trades around $130-$150 and then lifted off that level.
A brief consolidation around $250-$260 led to a bunch of institutional trades before another run to $300.

This follows a common pattern I see where institutions try to build the largest position possible during consolidation periods, but they try to build it as quietly as possible.

There were some very large trades around $300, including the 31st biggest trade ever, but the stock price struggled in that area.
It's possible that $300 might be a weak floor of support, but I'd bet on that level around $250 as a stronger floor.

## Thesis

This bears repeating: ***earnings are dangerous and highly unpredictable.***

If you do make an earnings play, keep it small and keep it fun.
You have about the same odds as winning the lottery. 🎟️

The cybersecurity sector is a strong one lately and CRWD is doing a good job of riding that wave.
The price action tells me right now that that traders are likely bullish but cautious due to the recent variance in cybersecurity sector earnings calls.

Long term indicators suggest that we're in for more risk to the upside but today's earnings call could change all of that.
