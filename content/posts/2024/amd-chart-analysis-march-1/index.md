AMD looks to be starting another run recently and it's a good time to check in on the chart.

NVDA seems to be getting most of the AI attention lately, but whenever I see a company with firm control over the market share of a particular product, I start looking for companies ready to disrupt that market.
AMD seems like it could be one of those.
Intel is in the running as well, but AMD's history makes me think they have what it takes.

{{< disclaimer >}}

Let's dig into some data.

## Long term look

A weekly chart since the last high in 2021 is a good place to start.
The original high of $165 from 2021 was tested thoroughly as support over the past five weeks.
Weekly candles have wicks dipping down to $165 and then running right back up again:

There are plenty of other bullish aspects in this chart:

* Price remains above VWAP lines drawn from the last two earnings calls
* Price also sits above the year to date (YTD) VWAP
* All three moving averages are properly stacked and moving higher
* The TTM squeeze and On Balance Volume (OBV) indicators show significant strength
* The Coppock Curve is flattening but is still well above the zero line

As I look at levels of support here, I'm considering a few different ones:

* $175 is right on the YTD VWAP line and there's also a decent amount of volume here
* $165 is an old high from 2021 that was once resistance and now appears to be support
* $160 is a low volume area that might be another level of support

Most of my plays are focused around 45-60 days to expiration (DTE), so this weekly chart works best for me.

## Institutional trades

[Volume Leaders](https://volumeleaders.com) provides insight into what big money traders are doing in the market.
These charts tell us when big trades were made, how large the trades were, and the price of the shares traded.

There's one critical thing to remember: **these charts don't tell us the direction of the trades.**
It's up to us to infer the direction with other analysis.

Here's a look at AMD over the past year:

{{< figure
    src="vl-1y.png"

There's a ton of consolidation around $100-$120 where institutions were making large trades as quietly as possible.
That led to a big rally where more large trades, including the 53rd biggest for AMD of all time, followed the price action.

Here's a look at the year to date chart:

{{< figure
    src="vl-ytd.png"

Now we see an area of consolidation around $160 to $185 where institutions again started making significant trades.
The blue circles are called "lit exchange trades" and they are regular trades made while the market is open.
Dark pool trades, or trades done off the exchanges and reported later, are the orange circles.

This is another example of institutions getting in as quietly as possible during a period of consolidation before another rally starts.
The activity centers heavily on $175 and this could be a good support level going forward.

## Thesis

Yes, AMD has been on an incredible run.

Yes, the entire semiconductor sector seems priced for perfection.

However, it's difficult to find anything in the charts or institutional data that suggests traders are turning bearish on AMD anytime soon.
We're also in uncharted territory as we reach new all time highs, so it's difficult to determine where the price action might stop or run into resistance.

As Tom Hougaard says, this is one of those situations where I plan to buy high and sell higher.
