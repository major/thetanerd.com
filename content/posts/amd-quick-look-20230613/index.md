# Vanna

Let's start with vanna:

{{< figure src="amd-vanna-curve.png" href="amd-vanna-curve.png" caption="AMD vanna curve from Volland" >}}

On aggregate, vanna is _still_ trending positive overall.
At the extremes on the vanna curve, the positive side is over **4x larger** than the negative side.
This is a bullish indicator as long as implied volatility (IV) remains low and/or steady.

How does each strike look?

{{< figure src="amd-vanna-bars.png" href="amd-vanna-bars.png" caption="AMD vanna bars from Volland" >}}

It's difficult to believe it, but there's nothing out there to slow down upward price movement between our current price ($131 pre-market) and $145.
The $145 strike dipped a bit yesterday but then came back towards the end of the day.
It's now just about even with the $110 strike's negative vanna.

Keep in mind that this runway for price really depends on where IV goes.
We have a lot of big announcements this week including CPI results and Federal Reserve meetings.

# Gamma

Let's get a look at Volland's delta-adjusted gamma (DAG) charts:

{{< figure src="amd-gamma-curve.png" href="amd-gamma-curve.png" caption="AMD gamma curve from Volland" >}}
{{< figure src="amd-gamma-bars.png" href="amd-gamma-bars.png" caption="AMD gamma bars from Volland" >}}

There's selling pressure across the board on the aggregate curve and it's finally at a magnitude worth paying attention to.
It's been fairly small recently relative to vanna, but we're approaching some vanna levels on aggregate.

These negative DAG levels above price could create some localized resistance for price movement.
However, that resistance really drops off past $130.
Our vanna chart above shows some strong buying pressure running through $145.

Although gamma is now large enough to warrant some extra attention, I still think vanna will win out on this one.

# Extras

Options flow looked interesting yesterday:

{{< figure src="amd-flow.png" href="amd-flow.png" caption="AMD options flow from tradytics" >}}

There was a large, sudden drop in put premium just after lunch.
This could be a buy back of long puts that were purchased as a hedge at some point.
It could also be evidence of a ton of put selling as IV crept up a little.

Speaking of IV:

{{< figure src="amd-chart.png" href="amd-chart.png" caption="AMD chart" >}}

AMD's IV crept up yesterday to about 54% but then slid back down closer to 50%.
The purple dashed line indicates the IV.
The dip into the 40-50% range ignited the recent rally that kicked off in early May.
You'll also notice that the recent consolidation occurred when IV crept up above 55%.

This will be something to watch out for this week with all the data published and quadruple witching on stock and index contracts.
