### Options data

My first look is on the vanna chart for 7/28. Vanna has weakened a bit (as it does in the second half of the month after OPEX), but it's worth a look:
![AMD vanna from Volland for 7/28](__GHOST_URL__/content/images/2023/07/image-81.png)AMD vanna from Volland for 7/28
We have positive vanna across the board here with a barely noticeable dip at $110. $109 is the biggest level (right on the support line) and it looks like selling puts from $106-$108 could be profitable this week. If we do go on a run, there's not much there to get us above $115.

☝️

Vanna effects on price **approach zero** as price approaches that level! Gamma effects on price are **at their highest** when price sits on a gamma level.

How does that continue into next week's earnings? Here's AMD's vanna for 8/4:
![AMD vanna from Volland for 8/4](__GHOST_URL__/content/images/2023/07/image-82.png)AMD vanna from Volland for 8/4
Let's assume we make a run to somewhere near $115 this week. What happens next week. Again, there's no fuel from vanna to exceed $115. It looks like the market is betting that AMD doesn't move much.

How about 8/11?
![AMD vanna from Volland on 8/11](__GHOST_URL__/content/images/2023/07/image-83.png)AMD vanna from Volland on 8/11
This looks less clear, but again, there's not much past $115 except for a little bit at $117 that is mostly canceled out by negative vanna right next door at $117.

How about the next OPEX date on 8/18 with tons of volume?
![AMD vanna from Volland on 8/18](__GHOST_URL__/content/images/2023/07/image-84.png)AMD vanna from Volland on 8/18
I like to see the $120 level growing here for 8/18, but $105 has suddenly appeared. Combined with the data for 8/11, there's a chance our $109 support could break down and potentially move to $105.

### What does this vanna data mean through 8/18?

The safest bets through 8/18 look like going bullish from $105-$110 and bearish past about $115. I'm going to watch that $120 level to see if it grows a bit more but this data screams price consolidation to me. Consolidation is a good thing, though, because it builds a base of support for future moves.
