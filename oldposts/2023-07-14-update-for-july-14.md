# AMD

Let's take a look at AMD first for a change. First up are vanna charts:

![](__GHOST_URL__/content/images/2023/07/Screenshot-2023-07-14-at-06-28-09-Vol.land-by-Wizard-of-Ops.png)

![](__GHOST_URL__/content/images/2023/07/Screenshot-2023-07-14-at-06-28-06-Vol.land-by-Wizard-of-Ops.png)

AMD vanna
AMD has finally moved into a pattern I really like to see:

- Plenty of positive vanna spread over a wide range *above price*
- Reduced positive vanna *below price*
- Vanna above price is 4x the vanna below price at the extremes
- Support from vanna around $110 remains

Bear in mind that vanna is weak on AMD right now and the tallest bar only reaches to about $25M. How about delta-adjusted gamma (DAG)?

![](__GHOST_URL__/content/images/2023/07/Screenshot-2023-07-14-at-06-30-09-Vol.land-by-Wizard-of-Ops.png)

![](__GHOST_URL__/content/images/2023/07/Screenshot-2023-07-14-at-06-30-06-Vol.land-by-Wizard-of-Ops.png)

AMD DAG
What a shift! We've been stuck in $110-$115 for ages with gamma slowing moves past $116-$120, but most of the selling pressure from gamma has disappeared. There's about $58M worth of gamma sitting underneath the current price that should provide support all the way down to $110.

Before we get too excited, there are some mixed signals here worth examining:

![](__GHOST_URL__/content/images/2023/07/Screenshot-2023-07-14-at-06-32-39-Flowline---Keep-your-Algo-Flows-Together.png)

![](__GHOST_URL__/content/images/2023/07/Screenshot-2023-07-14-at-06-32-37-Flowline---Keep-your-Algo-Flows-Together.png)

AMD net flow
AMD's flow flipped bearish on the 10th and remained there. Calls have sold off and they seem to sell off more aggressively each time AMD touches the $115-$116 zone. This could be profit taking on long calls, but it could also be a sign of bearish bets on AMD above $115.
![AMD dealer greeks on 15 day momentum](__GHOST_URL__/content/images/2023/07/image-36.png)AMD dealer greeks on 15 day momentum
Dealers are picking up bearish contracts *very, very slowly* which suggests that market participants are moving slightly more bullish each day, but not by large amounts. Seeing momentum shift like this for three days in a row is a good sign but I'd really like to see some taller red bars here.

What are the big options traders doing?
![Big options traders for AMD](__GHOST_URL__/content/images/2023/07/image-37.png)Big options traders for AMD
The $115 strike turned bearish on the 6th and it's a huge outlier on the chart. In addition, options are priced for a downward move of 2.4% or less.

# TSLA

TSLA earnings are coming up next week on July 19th, so be cautious here! Let's take a look at vanna:

![](__GHOST_URL__/content/images/2023/07/Screenshot-2023-07-14-at-06-37-47-Vol.land-by-Wizard-of-Ops.png)

![](__GHOST_URL__/content/images/2023/07/Screenshot-2023-07-14-at-06-37-45-Vol.land-by-Wizard-of-Ops.png)

TSLA vanna
The vanna curve is still very bullish, although I'd like to see more negative vanna below price. $280 is the largest level at $300M, but $290 and $300 are still fairly large. Bear in mind that as we approach $280, the vanna effect on price for the $280 strike falls off to zero. We will need something else to keep us going past that point.

With that in mind, let's look at delta-adjusted gamma:

![](__GHOST_URL__/content/images/2023/07/Screenshot-2023-07-14-at-06-39-52-Vol.land-by-Wizard-of-Ops.png)

![](__GHOST_URL__/content/images/2023/07/Screenshot-2023-07-14-at-06-39-49-Vol.land-by-Wizard-of-Ops.png)

TSLA DAG
Well, okay then, There's over $180M of selling pressure from gamma at $277.50 and $280. That's going to apply some heavy selling pressure at these levels that will be very localized. Remember that vanna has distant effects on price while gamma has more localized effects.

So far, we're looking at bullish vanna and a wall of bearish gamma just above the current price. How are options traders trading it?

![](__GHOST_URL__/content/images/2023/07/Screenshot-2023-07-14-at-06-41-52-Flowline---Keep-your-Algo-Flows-Together.png)

![](__GHOST_URL__/content/images/2023/07/Screenshot-2023-07-14-at-06-41-49-Flowline---Keep-your-Algo-Flows-Together.png)

TSLA options flow
Traders had some doubts on the 12th, but the flow is incredibly bullish over a five day period. What are dealers doing?
![TSLA dealer greeks buildup over 15 days](__GHOST_URL__/content/images/2023/07/Screenshot-2023-07-14-at-06-44-20-TSLA-Options-Dashboard-Dealer-Flows-Implied-Volatility-Algo-Flow.png)TSLA dealer greeks buildup over 15 days
**Oh no!** What happened here? 😱

We had a couple of days of dealers going short (customers bullish) just like AMD but then dealers suddenly went ***very, very long*** on TSLA yesterday. When I see this paired with a gamma wall at $280, I start to think we're hitting a top. TSLA is difficult to predict, but as a trader with a bullish bias, I don't like to see this.

Short interest is coming down on TSLA:

# Thesis

AMD has some improving data, but I'm still doubtful that it has a strong run above $115 in its short term future. Earnings run-ups can be wild, but I'm betting against that. I've covered all my shares from $115-$120 with short calls and pulled my short puts off the board. My cost basis is in the $90's, so an assignment in the upper $110's would be just fine with me. 😉

TSLA looks like it's approaching a top around $280, at least temporarily. I sold a $295 call for 7/21 and I'm leaving it on the board. I'd like to roll it down to $290 or $285, but I might just leave it where it is and take it off before earnings.
