Welcome back and good morning! 🌄 This post covers the latest data for TSLA and where it might be going in the coming weeks.

Lots of people are asking why we're in this bearish pattern. Well, one key point is yields on Treasury bonds are still quite high:
![](__GHOST_URL__/content/images/2023/10/image-14.png)
Why would you get into potentially risky equities when you get get 5% or more from an extremely low risk bond instead? Another issue in play is that the bond curve itself is quite inverted and it's becoming *more inverted* as time goes on.

On the surface, this means you'll get more yield on a shorter term bond than a longer term one. Dig deeper and you'll find that the chances of a recession increase (based on historical data) when this occurs. It doesn't happen *every time* but it does happen often.
![](__GHOST_URL__/content/images/2023/10/image-15.png)
The dollar is getting stronger, too.
![](__GHOST_URL__/content/images/2023/10/image-16.png)
All of these together put pressure on equities and send their prices down. Let's see how this is reflected in TSLA's, data. But first:

# Chart

TSLA's 4h chart shows that we're hanging out just below those two negative GEX levels at $250 and $255. TSLA is also resting on a trend line up from the September 27th low. It looks bullish on the 1 hour timeframe but all other timeframes have bearish tilts.

These bearish tilts are fairly light, though, as there's only one Change of Character (CHoCH) for each. Compare that to something like SPY where the 1 hour timeframe has **4 broken support levels in a row.** 👀

**If we break that trend line to the downside,** there's a bullish order block around $230-$235 which could provide support again. **If we follow the trend line up,** TSLA is likely going to be limited by the positive GEX at $260 and $265.
![](__GHOST_URL__/content/images/2023/10/TSLA_2023-10-04_06-35-29.png)
# Thesis

We're still in that mode where TSLA has the right things in place for a rally. There's decent support below price, we're trending up, and our bearish technicals are at their weakest. However, there's a a general move from the overall market to the downside and that will limit TSLA's upward options.
